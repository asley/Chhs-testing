# Assessment Recording Policy
## Internal Assessment and Markbook Usage Guidelines

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** December 1, 2025
**Review Date:** June 30, 2026
**Approved By:** Academic Leadership Team

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes clear guidelines for the use of Gibbon's Internal Assessment and Markbook systems to ensure:
- Consistent and accurate academic record-keeping
- Fair and transparent grading practices
- Compliance with institutional reporting requirements
- Effective communication of student progress to all stakeholders

---

## 2. Policy Statement

**Clement Howell High School requires all teaching staff to maintain accurate and timely records of student assessments using the appropriate Gibbon system based on the assessment type and purpose.**

All assessments contributing to:
- Final term/semester grades
- Year-end reports
- Prize giving and academic awards
- Official transcripts
- External reporting requirements

**MUST** be recorded in the **Internal Assessment** system.

---

## 3. System Definitions

### 3.1 Internal Assessment System

**Purpose:** Official academic record-keeping for summative assessments

**Characteristics:**
- Permanent academic records
- Standardized across all classes and departments
- Used for official reporting and transcripts
- Subject to administrative oversight and audit
- Aligned with school-wide grading policies
- Accessible to students and parents through official reports

**Data Integrity:** Internal Assessment records are considered official school documents and must be accurate, complete, and professionally maintained.

### 3.2 Markbook System

**Purpose:** Teacher's personal grade tracking tool for formative assessments and ongoing progress monitoring

**Characteristics:**
- Flexible and customizable by individual teachers
- Used for day-to-day classroom management
- Supports ongoing formative assessment
- Allows for weighted calculations and running averages
- Not used for official external reporting
- Teacher-controlled organization and structure

**Data Integrity:** While Markbook data is valuable for teaching and learning, it is not considered part of the official academic record.

## 4. Assessment Classification

### 4.1 MUST Use Internal Assessment

The following assessment types **MUST** be recorded in Internal Assessment:

#### Summative Assessments
- End-of-term examinations
- End-of-semester examinations
- Final examinations
- Standardized tests
- Major projects and coursework (weighted â‰¥15% of final grade)
- Mid-term assessments
- Unit tests (if contributing to final grade calculation)

#### Official Grade Components
- Any assessment contributing to final report card grades
- Assessments used for prize giving eligibility
- Assessments required for transcript generation
- Assessments used for promotion/retention decisions
- External examination results (CSEC, CAPE, etc.)

#### Assessment Columns Required per Term
**Minimum Requirements:**
- At least **THREE (3)** assessment entries per subject per term
- At least **ONE (1)** formal examination per term
- Assessment types must be clearly labeled (Exam, Test, Project, Coursework, etc.)

### 4.2 MAY Use Markbook

The following assessment types **MAY** be recorded in Markbook (optional but recommended):

#### Formative Assessments
- Daily homework assignments
- Quizzes (non-weighted or weighted <5%)
- Classwork and participation grades
- Practice exercises
- Warm-up activities
- Exit tickets
- Informal assessments

#### Progress Monitoring
- Running class averages
- Skills-based tracking
- Individual learning goal progress
- Behavioral observations
- Attendance and participation tracking

**Note:** Teachers are encouraged to use Markbook for ongoing progress monitoring and may choose to transfer significant formative assessment data to Internal Assessment if it contributes to summative evaluation.

## 5. Teacher Responsibilities

### 5.1 Data Entry Requirements

#### Timeliness
- **Internal Assessment entries:** Must be completed within **TEN (10) school days** of assessment completion
- **Major examinations:** Must be entered within **FIFTEEN (15) school days** of administration
- **End-of-term grades:** Must be finalized **FIVE (5) school days** before report card distribution

#### Accuracy
- All grades must be entered accurately and double-checked before finalization
- Assessment types must be correctly categorized (Exam, Test, Quiz, Project, etc.)
- Numeric grades must be entered as percentages (0-100)
- Comments should be professional and constructive
- Any errors must be corrected immediately and documented

#### Completeness
- All enrolled students must have grade entries for each assessment
- Missing assessments must be clearly marked (not left blank)
- Absences must be documented according to school policy
- Make-up assessment procedures must be followed

### 5.2 Assessment Column Setup

Teachers are responsible for:

1. **Creating Assessment Columns** in Internal Assessment at the beginning of each term
   - Use clear, descriptive names (e.g., "Term 1 Mid-Term Exam", "Unit 3 Project")
   - Specify the assessment type accurately
   - Set appropriate weighting if applicable
   - Include description of assessment criteria

2. **Communicating Assessment Plans** to students and parents
   - Share assessment calendar at term start
   - Clarify weighting and grading criteria
   - Provide rubrics for major assessments

3. **Maintaining Consistency** with department standards
   - Follow department-approved assessment frameworks
   - Use school-wide grading scales
   - Align with curriculum standards

### 5.3 Data Quality Standards

All assessment records must meet the following standards:

#### Professional Standards
- Use appropriate academic language
- Maintain objectivity and fairness
- Base grades on established criteria
- Document special circumstances appropriately

#### Technical Standards
- Use numeric percentages (not letter grades) for grade entry
- Enter data in the correct academic year and term
- Assign grades to the correct students
- Use the correct assessment column for each entry

#### Transparency Standards
- Assessment criteria must be shared with students in advance
- Grade calculations must be clear and verifiable
- Students must have access to their grades in a timely manner
- Parents must be able to view official grades through parent portal

### 5.4 Review and Verification

Teachers must:
- **Weekly:** Review Markbook entries for accuracy
- **Bi-weekly:** Verify Internal Assessment entries are up-to-date
- **End-of-term:** Audit all grades before final submission
- **Before reports:** Cross-check calculated averages for accuracy

### 5.5 Data Security and Privacy

Teachers are responsible for:
- Protecting student grade information confidentiality
- Not sharing grade information publicly
- Using secure methods for grade communication
- Following FERPA/data protection regulations
- Reporting any data breaches immediately to administration

## 7. Reporting and Grade Calculation

### 7.1 Official Grade Calculation

**Final term grades are calculated using:**
- **ONLY** data from Internal Assessment system
- Weighted according to assessment type (if applicable)
- Following department-approved calculation methods
- Subject to administrative verification

**Markbook data does NOT contribute to official grades** unless also entered into Internal Assessment.

### 7.2 Prize Giving and Awards

**Eligibility for academic awards is determined by:**
- Final averages calculated from Internal Assessment data
- Minimum number of assessments per subject (as specified above)
- Complete records for all enrolled courses
- Verified by GradeAnalytics module or authorized administrator

**Students are ineligible for prizes if:**
- They have incomplete Internal Assessment records
- Required assessments are missing
- Data entry deadlines were not met by teachers
- Data quality standards are not met

### 7.3 Report Card Generation

Official report cards will:
- Display grades from Internal Assessment only
- Show assessment types and dates
- Include teacher comments from Internal Assessment
- Reflect the official academic record

## 8. Student and Parent Access

### 8.1 Transparency

Students and parents have the right to:
- View Internal Assessment grades through the parent/student portal
- Request clarification of grading criteria
- Appeal grades through established procedures
- Access grade history and transcripts

### 8.2 Communication

Teachers should:
- Inform students of grades within one week of assessment
- Respond to grade inquiries within 48 hours
- Provide detailed feedback on major assessments
- Maintain open communication with parents/guardians

---

## 9. Special Circumstances

### 9.1 Students with Accommodations

Teachers must:
- Record accommodations in student notes
- Apply approved modifications consistently
- Document differentiated assessments
- Maintain confidentiality of accommodation details

### 9.2 Make-Up Assessments

When students miss assessments due to approved absences:
- Teachers must create and administer make-up assessments
- Make-up grades must be entered within 5 days of completion
- Original assessment dates should be noted in comments
- Make-up policies must be consistently applied

### 9.3 Grade Changes and Corrections

**Procedure for correcting errors:**
1. Identify the error and document the reason for change
2. Notify department head and academic coordinator
3. Make the correction in the system
4. Add a comment documenting the change
5. Notify the student/parent if the change affects their grade
6. Complete Grade Change Form (for post-report corrections)

**Grade changes after report distribution require:**
- Written justification
- Department Head approval
- Academic Coordinator notification
- Documentation in student file

## 12. Definitions

**Formative Assessment:** Ongoing assessments used to monitor student learning and provide feedback; typically low-stakes and used to inform instruction.

**Summative Assessment:** Assessments designed to evaluate student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against a standard or benchmark; typically high-stakes.

**Internal Assessment:** Gibbon's official academic record system for recording summative assessment data.

**Markbook:** Gibbon's flexible grade tracking tool for teacher use in monitoring ongoing student progress.

**Assessment Type:** Category of assessment (e.g., Exam, Test, Quiz, Project, Coursework, Homework).

**Official Academic Record:** Permanent record of student academic achievement used for transcripts, reports, and institutional decisions.

**Weighted Grade:** Grade calculation where different assessment types or components carry different percentages of the final grade.

**Grade Analytics Module:** Custom reporting module that calculates student averages and rankings from Internal Assessment data.

## Appendices

### Appendix A: Assessment Entry Checklist

**Before entering grades, verify:**
- [ ] Assessment column is created with correct name and type
- [ ] Assessment was administered to all students
- [ ] Grading criteria were applied consistently
- [ ] Grades are calculated accurately
- [ ] Special circumstances are documented
- [ ] Entry deadline will be met

**During grade entry:**
- [ ] Double-check student names match grades
- [ ] Use percentage format (0-100)
- [ ] Enter grades for ALL students (mark absences appropriately)
- [ ] Add comments where necessary
- [ ] Verify data before saving

**After grade entry:**
- [ ] Review for obvious errors or outliers
- [ ] Verify all students have entries
- [ ] Check calculations if using formulas
- [ ] Save and confirm submission
- [ ] Notify students of their grades

### Appendix B: Assessment Type Guidelines

| Assessment Type | Definition | Examples | Typical Weight |
|----------------|------------|----------|----------------|
| **Exam** | Comprehensive formal test covering major content | End-of-term, Mid-term, Final | 20-40% |
| **Test** | Formal assessment of specific unit/topic | Unit test, Chapter test | 10-20% |
| **Quiz** | Brief assessment of recent content | Pop quiz, Weekly quiz | 5-10% |
| **Project** | Extended work demonstrating application | Research paper, Presentation | 15-30% |
| **Coursework** | Ongoing assignments and activities | Lab reports, Essays | 10-20% |
| **Homework** | Practice assignments completed outside class | Problem sets, Reading responses | 5-15% |

### Appendix C: Common Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Student absent for major exam**
- Action: Mark as absent in Internal Assessment
- Follow school make-up assessment policy
- Administer make-up within required timeframe
- Enter make-up grade with note

**Scenario 2: Discovered grading error after reports sent**
- Action: Complete Grade Change Form
- Get Department Head approval
- Submit to Academic Coordinator
- Update Internal Assessment with documentation
- Notify student/parent of correction

**Scenario 3: Student transfers mid-term**
- Action: Ensure all completed assessments are entered
- Mark remaining assessments as N/A or transfer
- Provide grade summary to receiving school
- Archive records per retention policy

**Scenario 4: Technical system error during entry**
- Action: Screenshot the error
- Contact IT Help Desk immediately
- Document what data was lost
- Re-enter when system is restored
- Request deadline extension if needed
